Computerized Composite Wall Apparatus

The Bluestar Equipment Computerized Composite Wall Apparatus is designed to provide comprehensive experiments for teaching heat conduction through various metals. The apparatus uses one of eleven samples, where the upper region is heated by an electrical heater, and the lower section is cooled by a Peltier element. Heat conduction occurs through the sample from top to bottom, allowing for the investigation of thermal conductivity in multi-layered metals. The well-matched components ensure rapid heating and precise measurements.

The temperature is measured using thermocouples placed at the top and bottom of the metal samples. The microprocessor-based instrumentation is securely housed, and the software includes system operation, data acquisition, and educational content with explanatory texts and illustrations to aid understanding. The unit connects to a PC via USB.
