Computerized Composite Wall Apparatus

Model No:HMT-003

Description:

The Computerized Composite Wall Apparatus by Bluestar is designed to facilitate fundamental experiments on heat conduction through various metals. This apparatus enables users to study heat transfer using one of eleven metal samples. The upper section of each sample is heated by an electrical heater, while the lower section is cooled by a Peltier element. Heat conduction occurs from top to bottom through the sample. The unit can accommodate two samples simultaneously, allowing for the investigation of thermal conductivity in multi-layered metal configurations. Its precisely engineered components ensure rapid heating and accurate measurements.

Temperature readings are obtained from the top and bottom of the metal samples using thermocouples. The apparatus features a microprocessor-based system housed in a protective enclosure. The included software consists of system operation tools, data acquisition features, and educational software, which provides comprehensive explanations and illustrations to enhance theoretical understanding. The unit connects to a PC via USB for seamless integration.
